Passwords

Passwords are the most common form of security. Ever since its inventor, Fernando Corbato devised the idea in the early 1960s, it has been universally used to secure anything and everything. The security a password provides relies on the password set by the user which makes it vulnerable by nature. A weak password is prone to an attack that everyone has tried- trial and error; which is referred to as brute force and make up 13% of the top network attacks.

The way we set up our passwords now is different from the way it was 50 years ago. Our passwords are forced to be a certain length, as well as a mixture of uppercase, lowercase and non-alphanumeric characters to decrease the chance of it being guessed.

Attackers can gather details about the target, enabling them to access their account through brute force. Two-factor authentication (TFA) or multi-step authentication serves as an extra layer of security, it is a physical token that the user has access to, such as an external device in which the account is authenticated. Two-factor authentication can also be as convenient as entering information only known by the user, although it is not as secure as a physical device.

A flaw in physical tokens is that they may be lost, since they are small it will take time and money to reissue it, forcing users to wait. Tokenless authentication is a development patented by SecurEnvoy. It provides users an alternative to physical devices in which the account will be authenticated via an app or SMS.
